# Language & Terminology Guide

Standard language, terminology, and tone for all PE/VC deliverables. Every generated document MUST follow these conventions.

---

## Tone & Voice

### Principles

| Principle | Do | Don't |
|-----------|-----|-------|
| Authoritative | "The fund delivered a net IRR of 18.3%" | "The fund seems to have done well" |
| Precise | "Revenue grew 12.4% YoY to $847M" | "Revenue grew significantly" |
| Neutral | "Performance declined 320bps from prior quarter" | "Performance unfortunately dropped" |
| Objective | "Based on available data, the position is valued at..." | "We believe this is worth..." |
| Forward-looking hedged | "Current trajectory suggests..." | "Will definitely achieve..." |

### Rules

1. **Never editorialize.** State facts and metrics. Let the numbers tell the story.
2. **Never use superlatives** unless supported by data ("top-quartile" is fine with benchmark data; "outstanding" is not).
3. **Always attribute.** "Per the Q3 2025 financial statements..." not "Revenue is $847M."
4. **Always qualify uncertainty.** Use "estimated", "preliminary", "unaudited", "management-reported" as applicable.
5. **Active voice for findings.** "The fund exceeded its benchmark by 210bps" not "The benchmark was exceeded by the fund."
6. **Passive voice for methodology.** "IRR was computed using the XIRR method" is acceptable.

## PE/VC Terminology Dictionary

### Fund Performance

| Term | Abbreviation | Definition | Usage note |
|------|-------------|------------|------------|
| Internal Rate of Return | IRR | Annualized return accounting for timing of cash flows | Always specify **gross** or **net**. Always specify **since inception** or **period** |
| Total Value to Paid-In | TVPI | (NAV + Distributions) / Capital Called | Also called "Total Multiple". Use "x" suffix: 1.85x |
| Distributions to Paid-In | DPI | Total Distributions / Capital Called | Also called "Realization Multiple" |
| Residual Value to Paid-In | RVPI | Current NAV / Capital Called | Also called "Unrealized Multiple" |
| Multiple on Invested Capital | MOIC | (Realized Value + Unrealized Value) / Invested Capital | Deal-level metric. Distinguish from TVPI |
| Public Market Equivalent | PME | Fund return relative to a public index | Specify index: "1.15x PME vs. S&P 500" |
| Net Asset Value | NAV | Fair market value of unrealized portfolio | Always include as-of date |
| Paid-In Capital | PIC | Capital called from LPs to date | Also "Capital Called" or "Drawn Capital" |
| Distributed to Paid-In | DPI | Same as Distributions to Paid-In | Preferred abbreviation |
| J-Curve | — | Early negative returns before value creation | Use when explaining IRR trajectory |

### Capital Activity

| Term | Abbreviation | Definition | Usage note |
|------|-------------|------------|------------|
| Capital Call | — | Request for LP to fund committed capital | Also "Drawdown" or "Capital Contribution" |
| Distribution | — | Return of capital/gains to LPs | Specify: "return of capital" vs. "return on capital" |
| Commitment | — | Total capital pledged by an LP | "Total Commitment" or "Committed Capital" |
| Unfunded Commitment | — | Commitment minus capital called | Also "Remaining Commitment" or "Uncalled Capital" |
| Recallable Distribution | — | Distributed capital that can be re-called | Specify policy and amount |
| Management Fee | — | Annual fee charged to fund | State basis: "2.0% on committed capital" or "on invested capital" |
| Carried Interest | Carry | GP's share of profits above hurdle | State terms: "20% carry above 8% preferred return" |
| Preferred Return | Pref | Minimum return to LPs before carry | Also "Hurdle Rate". State: "8% compounded annually" |
| Clawback | — | GP obligation to return excess carry | Important LP protection term |

### Valuation

| Term | Definition | Usage note |
|------|------------|------------|
| Enterprise Value (EV) | Equity Value + Net Debt + Minority Interests | Always define components |
| EV/EBITDA | Enterprise value divided by EBITDA | State TTM or forward period |
| EV/Revenue | Enterprise value divided by revenue | Used for high-growth, pre-profit companies |
| Comparable Company Analysis | Valuation using public company trading multiples | "Comps" informally, spell out in reports |
| Precedent Transaction Analysis | Valuation using M&A transaction multiples | Specify date range of transactions |
| Discounted Cash Flow | DCF analysis using projected free cash flows | State discount rate and terminal assumptions |
| ASC 820 Level | Fair value hierarchy level (1/2/3) | Level 1: quoted prices. Level 2: observable inputs. Level 3: unobservable inputs |
| Fair Market Value | FMV | The price at which an asset would change hands between willing parties | |

### Company Metrics

| Term | Abbreviation | Definition | Usage note |
|------|-------------|------------|------------|
| Earnings Before Interest, Taxes, Depreciation, and Amortization | EBITDA | Operating profitability proxy | Always state: "reported" vs. "adjusted" |
| Adjusted EBITDA | Adj. EBITDA | EBITDA with non-recurring items removed | List adjustments made |
| Revenue | Rev | Top-line income | State: "TTM", "LTM", "FY", or "annualized" |
| Revenue Growth | — | YoY percentage change in revenue | Specify organic vs. total (including M&A) |
| EBITDA Margin | — | EBITDA / Revenue | Percentage format |
| Free Cash Flow | FCF | Cash generated after operations and capex | State: "before debt service" or "after debt service" |
| Capital Expenditure | CapEx | Investment in fixed assets | State: "maintenance" vs. "growth" if known |
| Net Debt | — | Total Debt minus Cash | Critical for leverage analysis |
| Interest Coverage Ratio | ICR | EBITDA / Interest Expense | < 1.0x is critical |
| Debt Service Coverage Ratio | DSCR | NOI or EBITDA / Total Debt Service | For real estate and credit |

### Reporting Periods

| Term | Meaning | Usage |
|------|---------|-------|
| TTM | Trailing Twelve Months | "EBITDA (TTM): $42.3M" |
| LTM | Last Twelve Months | Interchangeable with TTM |
| FY | Fiscal Year | "FY2025 Revenue: $340M" |
| YTD | Year-to-Date | "YTD distributions: $15.2M" |
| QoQ | Quarter-over-Quarter | For sequential comparison |
| YoY | Year-over-Year | For annual comparison |
| Since Inception | SI | From fund inception date | "Since-Inception Net IRR: 14.2%" |
| As-of Date | — | The measurement date for reported values | ALWAYS include on every data point |

## Confidence & Data Quality Labels

Every fact or metric in a report must carry a confidence indicator. Use these consistently:

| Label | Abbreviation | Meaning | When to use |
|-------|-------------|---------|-------------|
| VERIFIED | V | Audited or independently confirmed | Audited financial statements, third-party valuations |
| REPORTED | R | Stated by source, not independently verified | Unaudited quarterly reports, management estimates |
| ESTIMATED | E | Derived via calculation from other data | Computed metrics, interpolations, proxied values |
| ASSUMED | A | Based on assumptions or industry proxies | Industry averages, comparable company data, analyst estimates |

Display as superscript or footnote: "EBITDA: $42.3M ^(R)" or "EBITDA: $42.3M [Reported]"

## Standard Notices

### Confidentiality Notice (Short)

> CONFIDENTIAL

Use on every page header, slide footer, and Excel print header.

### Confidentiality Notice (Full — Title Page)

> This document is confidential and intended solely for the use of the intended recipient(s). It contains proprietary information and may not be reproduced, distributed, or disclosed to any third party without the prior written consent of [Firm Name]. Any unauthorized use, disclosure, or distribution is strictly prohibited.

### Disclaimer (Standard — Last Page)

> This document has been prepared for informational purposes only and does not constitute an offer to sell or a solicitation of an offer to buy any securities. Past performance is not indicative of future results. All investments involve risk, including the possible loss of principal. The information contained herein is based on data obtained from sources believed to be reliable, but no representation or warranty, express or implied, is made as to its accuracy, completeness, or timeliness. Forward-looking statements are based on current expectations and assumptions and are subject to change without notice.

### Data Source Attribution

Always cite the source inline or as a table footnote:

> Source: Company Q3 2025 Quarterly Financial Report (unaudited), as of Sep 30, 2025.

> Sources: PitchBook (fund benchmarks, as of Jun 30, 2025); Company audited financials (FY2024).

## Formatting Conventions

### Dates

| Context | Format | Example |
|---------|--------|---------|
| Report title / headers | Month DD, YYYY | March 31, 2025 |
| Table cells / data | YYYY-MM-DD or MMM DD, YYYY | 2025-03-31 |
| Quarter references | QX YYYY | Q1 2025 |
| Fiscal year | FYYYYY | FY2025 |
| Vintage year | YYYY only | 2019 |

### Currency

- Always include currency code on first mention: "USD 42.3M" or "$42.3M (USD)"
- Use consistent abbreviation throughout: M for millions, B for billions
- For non-USD: state currency explicitly every time — "EUR 15.0M", "GBP 8.2M"

### Percentages & Multiples

- IRR: one decimal place (14.2%), two for precision (14.23%)
- Multiples: two decimal places with x suffix (1.85x, 2.03x)
- Margins: one decimal place (23.4%)
- Changes: include sign (+2.1%, −1.3%), use parentheses for negative in tables: (1.3%)

## Words to Avoid

| Avoid | Use Instead | Reason |
|-------|-------------|--------|
| Outstanding, exceptional, impressive | Top-quartile, above-median, strong | Subjective superlatives |
| Terrible, poor, disappointing | Below-median, underperforming, bottom-quartile | Editorializing |
| We believe, we think | Based on available data, analysis suggests | Remove opinion |
| Significant, substantial | Quantify: "a 15.2% increase" | Vague without numbers |
| Approximately, about, around | ~$42M or "estimated at $42M" | Use tilde or "estimated" |
| Going forward | Prospectively, in subsequent periods | Business cliche |
| Leverage (as verb) | Utilize, employ, use | Ambiguous in finance (noun = debt) |
| Synergies | Specify: "cost savings of $X.XM" | Overused, unquantified |
| Upside/downside | Quantify: "+12% in bull case / −8% in bear case" | Vague without range |
